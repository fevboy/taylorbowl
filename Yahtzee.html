Yahtzee
This page is under construction. We are currently looking for images to add to it. Please DM ev on Discord if you have any images that may be worthwhile. Thank you!
Yahtzee is a dice-based minigame. Five whole numbers from 1 to 6 are randomly generated, and certain combinations will award the player credits. Users each get some Free Yahtzees in each Bot Restart, and can use Paid Yahtzees once the free ones have been used up which cost 10 credits each.
Users may use the command !yahtzee number to play Yahtzee, or the command !yahtzee all to use as many rolls as possible within Discord's character limitation. Note that this will also use a user's paid rolls, and can reduce their credits to negative. Users have some free rolls each Bot Cycle. Every Server Booster grants 1.5 free rolls, rounded up. After all free rolls have been used, each subsequent paid roll costs 10 credits. Each player has a limited number of paid rolls per Bot Cycle. This number starts at 150 and is increased by 150 for every Ephemera Level.

There are four types of combinations that will award credits.
  1. 1-Roll Yahtzee: All five numbers are the same. This awards 792x the number shown on all dice.
  2. 4-of-a-Kind: Four of five numbers are the same. This awards 48x the number shown on the four dice.
  3. Full House: Three dice share one number, and the other two dice share another number. This awards 3x the sum of all numbers shown.
  4. Large Straight: The dice show a consecutive sequence of numbers when rearranged, i.e., they show the numbers 1 2 3 4 5 or 2 3 4 5 6 in any order. This awards 3x of the sum of all numbers shown.
The expected value of a free Yahtzee is 9.0255, and the expected value of a paid Yahtzee is -0.9745, shown by:

$$E(\text{1-Roll Yahtzee})=\sum_{i=1}^{6}792i\cdot\left(\cfrac{1}{6}\right)^{5}\approx2.1389$$ $$E(\text{4-of-a-Kind})=\sum_{i=1}^{6}48i\cdot\left(\cfrac{1}{6}\right)^{4}\cdot\cfrac{5}{6}\approx3.2407$$ $$E(\text{Full House})=\sum_{i=1}^{6}\sum_{j=1}^{6}\left(3(3i+2j)-5\sum_{k=1}^{6}k\right)\cdot\cfrac{5!}{3!\cdot2!}\cdot\left(\cfrac{1}{6}\right)^{5}\approx2.0255$$ $$E(\text{Large Straight})=\left(\cfrac{1}{6}\right)^{5}\cdot5!\cdot3\cdot\left(\sum_{i=1}^{5}i+\sum_{j=2}^{6}j\right)\approx1.6204$$ $$\sum E(X)=9.0255$$ $$\sum E(X)-10=-0.9745$$

There is 1 Invocation that affects Yahtzees.
Discount
Discount is used to reduce the invoker's Paid Yahtzee cost. The command !discount consumes up to 8 Ephemera to reduce the cost of each Paid Yahtzee by the same number of credits, i.e. invoking Discount with 5 Ephemera decreases the cost of each Paid Yahtzee by 5 credits. If the invoker has 8 or less Ephemera, all will be consumed with no exceptions. If the invoker has 9 or more Ephemera, 8 will be consumed and the excess will remain on-hand. The effect of Discount is reset by Bot Restarts. Discount can only be used once per Bot Cycle.

Discount will misfire if the invoker has already used Discount during the Bot Cycle, consuming Ephemera as if it had been successfully used, i.e. all Ephemera on-hand or 8, whichever is lesser.

Since the expected value of a free Yahtzee is 9.0255, by Discounting once, you are expected to make a small margin of profit. However, by discounting using the maximum of 8 Ephemera, the expected value becomes 7.0255. This makes Yahtzee a viable method for obtaining credits, given a user has sufficient Ephemera and a large enough number of paid rolls. The total expected returns on Yahtzees is given by:

$$\text{returns}=n(9.0255-c)=n(d-0.9745)$$

where \(n\) is the number of rolls, \(c\) is the cost per roll, and \(d\) is the number of Discounts used.
